Decimal Expansion of Rational Numbers

Rational numbers can be expressed in the form p/q where q is not equal to zero. One important property of rational numbers is that their decimal expansions either terminate after a finite number of digits or continue infinitely in a repeating pattern. Decimal expansion helps students convert fractions into decimal form for easier comparison, computation, and real world application. This concept is widely used in science, finance, engineering, programming, and statistics where numerical precision and decimal representation are essential. Understanding decimal expansion strengthens arithmetic skills and improves conceptual clarity regarding fractions, divisions, recurring patterns, and numerical representation in mathematics and computational systems.
Terminating decimals end after a fixed number of digits, such as 1/2 = 0.5, while non terminating recurring decimals repeat a specific sequence infinitely, such as 1/3 = 0.333.... Decimal expansion is highly useful in calculators, digital systems, banking software, measurement systems, and scientific computations where fractions are commonly converted into decimals. The study of decimal representation also helps students understand continuity between fractions and real numbers. This topic creates a strong mathematical foundation for algebra, coordinate geometry, data analysis, and advanced computational mathematics. Modern educational technologies and numerical algorithms extensively depend on decimal expansion concepts for precise calculations and efficient digital processing.
Decimal Expansion Examples:

1/2 = 0.5 → Terminating Decimal

3/4 = 0.75 → Terminating Decimal

1/3 = 0.3333... → Non Terminating Recurring Decimal

2/11 = 0.181818... → Recurring Pattern

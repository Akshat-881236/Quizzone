Prime and Composite Numbers

Prime and composite numbers are important classifications in mathematics that help students understand number properties and factorization concepts. A prime number is a number greater than one that has only two factors: one and itself. Composite numbers are numbers that have more than two factors. Prime numbers are considered the building blocks of mathematics because every composite number can be expressed as a product of prime numbers. Understanding prime and composite numbers helps students improve arithmetic operations, factorization skills, and logical reasoning abilities. These concepts form the foundation for higher mathematical topics such as algebra, cryptography, number theory, and computational mathematics used in modern educational systems.
Prime and composite numbers are widely used in computer science, encryption systems, cybersecurity technologies, banking systems, and digital communications. Modern internet security systems often depend on large prime numbers to protect confidential information and secure online transactions. Students who understand these number classifications develop stronger analytical thinking and improved problem solving skills. Prime numbers are also important in coding algorithms, artificial intelligence systems, and advanced mathematical research. Composite numbers help students learn factorization techniques and divisibility concepts that are essential for solving equations and performing complex calculations in engineering, software development, scientific analysis, and modern technological applications across digital infrastructures worldwide.
Prime Numbers → 2,3,5,7,11,13...

Composite Numbers → 4,6,8,9,10,12...
